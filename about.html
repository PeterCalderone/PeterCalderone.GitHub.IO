Root Overview

Rooting is the Android equivalent of jailbreaking, unlocking the OS allows you to install apps that require access to certain system settings, update the OS, replace the firmware, overclock (or underclock) the processor, and customize just about anything.

Custom Recovery

Custom recoveries allow you to install custom ROMs, Kernels, and replace stock firmware. TWRP Recovery is the most popular and user-friendly for restoring backups created and flashing any flashable zip file.

Custom ROMs

A custom ROM replaces your device's stock OS — normally stored in read-only memory — with a new version of the Android operating system, like CM13 (see below). Installing a Custom ROM is different from rooting your Android device!

CyanogenMod 13

CyanogenMod 13 (or CM13) is their latest firmware release based around Android Marshmallow. Though for most devices, CM13 has not reached it's stable stages yet, that is expected to arrive around mid-April 2016

Why CyanogenMod?

CyanogenMod is secure, light-footprint, bloat-free, and open to vast customization. Without these features, you never truly own your phone.

CM Themes

For some, this may be the most intriguing part of CyanogenMod firmware. You can change the look and feel of the entire OS through the pre-installed app that's downloaded along with the ROM. Even better, you can download more themes from the Google Play Store
